Motivation

On this page you will find my research of what is motivation and how its theory applies to me - how motivated I am.

Theory

What exaclty is motivation? What types of motivation can be distinguished?

Definition

There are a lot of different and slightly different definitions of motivation. Accoding to Kendra Cherry, motivation is the process that initiates, guides, and maintains goal-oriented behaviors (Kendra Cherry, 2020). In other words, it is the reason why people do things that lead to accomplishing certain goals. A person could spend time watching TV, but instead, he spent it writting a book, why? Because of motivation.

Types of motivation

People can be motivated to do things because of different reasons. Phychologists distinguish two types of motivation, based on where these reasons come from.

  1. Extrinsic motivation. When a person is extrinsically motivated, he does the work because it leads him to some sort of result - money, awards, or work result itself. A person might not enjoy what he is doing, but he will do it, for external reasons. (David Burkus, 2020)
  2. Intrinsic motivation. That is when person does work because he enjoys doing it. It comes from the inside of a body, not from an external motivating factor. (David Burkus, 2020)

Dan Pink about motivation

Pink talked about differences in people performance if they are extrinsically motivated, or intrinsically motivated.

Extrinsic motivation, or the "carrot and stick" approach , is a good, effective and working method to motivate people do simple and straightforward tasks, like carry 5 fridges to the 8th floor - if you would get 20 dollars for that you would do it more effectively.

But this approach doesn't work for most of the tasks of 21th century, since they require thinking out-of-the-box. Experiments have shown that extrinsic motivation blocks creativity, which is the only important thing nowadays, because most of non-creative tasks have already been automated. The solution is to make people have intrinsic motivation, so they do things because they like it. (Dan Pink, 2009)

My conclusion

About applying intrinsic motivation rather than extrinsic, Daniel talked on the level of organizations, companies and business. However, anology can also be driven to a personal level. Since studying and programming is not a straighforward and easy process, it is better to have intrinsic motivation for it.
